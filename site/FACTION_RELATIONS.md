## Detailed Relations

### United Earth Federation (UEF)

**Allies:**
- **Republic of Mars**: Historical tensions resolved, now strong military and economic alliance

**Neutral:**
- **Free Traders Guild**: Economic partnership, mutual benefit
- **Frontier Worlds**: Leave them alone, they leave us alone
- **Auroran Empire**: Cautious diplomacy, no formal relations

**Enemies:**
- **Crimson Collective**: Active military operations to suppress piracy

**Stance:**
- Defensive posture
- Law and order focus
- Diplomatic first, military when necessary
- Seeks to expand influence peacefully

---

### Republic of Mars (ROM)

**Allies:**
- **United Earth Federation**: Close military cooperation

**Neutral (with all others):**
- Focused on industrial dominance
- Sells to anyone (except Crimson during wars)
- No territorial ambitions outside Sol system

**Stance:**
- Isolationist except for trade
- Proud independence
- Military strength through superior technology
- Profit-driven foreign policy

---

### Free Traders Guild (FTG)

**Enemies:**
- **Crimson Collective**: Pirates threaten trade routes

**Neutral (with others):**
- Strict neutrality in conflicts
- Trade with all legitimate governments
- Will not take sides in wars

**Stance:**
- Profit above all
- Neutrality is good for business
- Hire protection, don't fight directly
- Open markets benefit everyone

---

### Frontier Worlds Alliance (FWA)

**Enemies:**
- **Crimson Collective**: Frequent raids on frontier colonies

**Neutral (with others):**
- Don't want to be drawn into core politics
- Cooperate when mutually beneficial
- Suspicious of core governments

**Stance:**
- Independence and self-reliance
- Mutual defense only
- Trade when profitable
- Resist external control

---

### Crimson Collective

**Enemies (with everyone except Auroran):**
- Attack any target of opportunity
- Declared terrorists by UEF
- Hunted by FTG escorts
- Raid frontier colonies

**Neutral:**
- **Auroran Empire**: Too dangerous to mess with

**Stance:**
- Survival through predation
- Freedom from government control
- Might makes right
- No honor among thieves (but loyalty to crew)

---

### Auroran Empire

**Neutral (with all):**
- Observing humanity
- Minimal contact
- Motives unknown

**Stance:**
- Isolationist
- Technologically superior
- Defensive of territory
- Inscrutable

## Conflict Scenarios

### Active Wars
*None at game start, but can be triggered*

### Cold Wars
1. **UEF vs Crimson**: Ongoing anti-piracy operations
2. **UEF vs ROM** (Historical): Resolved, but tensions can reignite

### Potential Conflicts

**Scenario 1: Second Martian Crisis**
- ROM grows too powerful
- UEF feels threatened
- Players choose sides
- Could split player base

**Scenario 2: Frontier Uprising**
- FWA unites against core control
- Attacks on UEF outposts
- Crimson joins opportunistically
- Chaos in outer systems

**Scenario 3: Crimson Consolidation**
- Pirates unite under strong leader
- Declare "Free State"
- Seize multiple systems
- All factions forced to respond

**Scenario 4: Auroran Incursion**
- Aurorans suddenly expand
- Attack or assimilate systems
- Humanity must unite or fall
- Endgame crisis event

**Scenario 5: Economic Collapse**
- FTG manipulation causes crash
- All factions destabilized
- Opportunity for player factions
- New order emerges

## Player Impact on Relations

### Actions That Affect Faction Relations

**Positive Actions:**
- Complete faction missions (+5 to +25 rep)
- Trade legally in their space (+1 rep per 100k credits)
- Defeat their enemies (+10 rep per kill)
- Discover new routes to their systems (+20 rep)
- Donate to faction (+1 rep per 10k credits)

**Negative Actions:**
- Attack faction ships (-50 rep per kill)
- Smuggle contraband in their space (-10 rep if caught)
- Fail missions (-20 rep)
- Attack their allies (-25 rep)
- Aid their enemies (-15 rep per enemy mission)

### Reputation Thresholds

**Beloved (75+):**
- Special missions unlocked
- 20% trading discount
- Free refueling
- Priority docking
- Faction will come to aid if attacked

**Respected (50-74):**
- High-level missions
- 10% trading discount
- Loan access
- Preferred partner

**Friendly (25-49):**
- Most missions available
- 5% discount
- Good standing

**Liked (10-24):**
- Standard missions
- Normal prices
- Welcome in their space

**Neutral (-9 to 9):**
- Basic missions only
- Standard prices
- No special treatment

**Disliked (-24 to -10):**
- Few missions
- 10% markup
- Watched closely

**Unfriendly (-49 to -25):**
- No missions
- 20% markup
- May refuse docking

**Hostile (-74 to -50):**
- Attacked if carrying contraband
- 50% markup (if they trade at all)
- Unwelcome

**Hated (-75 to -100):**
- Attacked on sight
- Cannot dock
- Kill on sight orders

## Faction War Mechanics

### Declaration of War
- Requires major provocation or player actions
- Announced server-wide
- Creates combat zones

### War Zones
- Border systems become battlefields
- NPC fleets engage each other
- Players can choose sides
- Reputation gains/losses amplified

### War Missions
- Fleet battles (large scale)
- System defense/attack
- Supply runs (high pay, high risk)
- Espionage and sabotage

### War Economy
- War materials in high demand
- Prices spike for weapons, fuel, repairs
- Trade routes disrupted
- Opportunity for smugglers

### War Resolution
- One side gains territory
- Peace treaty signed
- Reputation resets partially
- New political landscape

## Joining NPC Factions (Future Feature)

### Requirements
- 50+ Reputation
- Complete faction storyline
- Oath of loyalty
- Restrictions applied

### Benefits
- Faction ships/equipment
- Salary
- Special missions
- Protected status

### Restrictions
- Cannot attack faction or allies
- Must respond to calls for aid
- Limited freedom
- Can lose membership

## Player Faction Relations with NPCs

Player factions have separate reputation from individual members:

### Benefits of Good Standing
- Faction missions (whole faction participates)
- Bulk trade discounts
- Territory recognition
- Military support

### Consequences of Bad Standing
- Faction banned from space
- All members suffer penalties
- Territory attacked
- Bounties on members

### Neutral Stance
- Most player factions start neutral
- Must actively work to gain rep
- Can remain neutral (Switzerland approach)

## Dynamic Faction Changes

### Faction Strength
- Changes based on:
  - Player mission completions
  - Economic performance
  - Military victories/defeats
  - Territory control

### Border Shifts
- Factions can gain/lose systems
- Affects trade routes
- Changes strategic landscape

### Alliance Shifts
- Can change based on events
- ROM and UEF could split
- FWA could join UEF if threatened
- Crimson could be legitimized

### Faction Collapse (Rare)
- If faction loses all territory
- Members scatter
- Power vacuum
- Opportunity for players
